Branding Project

Introduction

BLIK is a group of women who want to express what playback theatre is and the way my group and I will have to do this is by creating a brand identity for them. The brand identity will be used to promote the group and their performances. From here I will explain the work I did for the group and the process of my parts in creating the brand identity.

Tools Used

  • Word
  • Figma
  • Google Forms
  • Powerpoint
  • Photoshop

Process

This project involved creating a brand identity for BLIK Playback Theatre by targeting HR professionals, event organizers, and private clients as Blik's target audience. To get the data collection process started I conducted a survey to get a better understanding of what the target audience would like to see in this brand identity. A competitor analysis followed, comparing similar playback theatre groups in terms of their social media presence, engagement, and website functionality. The findings were compiled into a model on Figma for reference in the brand guide.

At first, I had questions made on Google Forms but after some feedback I changed the questions asked to fit better for the target group of companies, event organizers and private individuals. Also, the structure on how the survey should be answered as at first majority of the questions would needed to be answered openly as I was assuming that the answers were be more descriptive than a simple one to two word answer because I was first aiming to theatre enjoyers as the target group. From then on I switched to google forms to make more multiple choice questions, and likert scale type questions, to have the survey takers be more engaged with simple clicks and images.

Target Demographic: The target audience is mainly 25-35-year-olds, with a large portion working as finance officers.
Familiarity with Playback Theatre: Most respondents were unfamiliar with Playback Theatre, underscoring the need for content that explains the concept clearly and emphasizes its benefits to potential clients.
Content Strategy: The preferred content types are highlight reels, behind-the-scenes content, and promotions for upcoming events. These will form the core of the client’s social media strategy, with a recommended posting frequency of twice a week for optimal engagement.
Visual and Website Preferences: Respondents favored website content that includes show details, booking rates, and the group’s mission and values. They preferred imagery of emotional moments from performances and group photos of actors interacting with the audience, reflecting the essence of Playback Theatre.
Colors and Fonts: Dark, moody colors were associated with deep emotions, though it would be better to align color and font choices with the client’s core values.
Booking Motivations: Emotional impact and team-building benefits were identified as the primary factors influencing booking decisions.
Social Media Preferences: Instagram and Facebook are the most used platforms by the target audience. Posts will be scheduled for evenings when engagement is highest, focusing on these two platforms.

Like mentioned before, I looked onto these theatre groups as they also work on playback theatre and they are few of the mentioned groups we can look into by Blik. This is the summary of the findings I have made on the groups and how they are doing on social media and their websites.

Theater Draad
Theater Draad primarily uses Instagram, Facebook, and LinkedIn, but they have an irregular posting schedule with updates every 1-3 months. Their social media content mainly features group photos, performance setups, and event announcements without a consistent color scheme or visual identity. Audience engagement is minimal, with only occasional likes on comments and little direct interaction. Their website is functional, mobile-responsive, and easy to navigate, offering features such as event details, a performance request form, and a newsletter sign-up. However, the overall design lacks a distinctive appeal.

TTA (Terugspeeltheater Amsterdam)
TTA's social media presence includes Twitter, Facebook, and LinkedIn, but they post inconsistently, often skipping months. Their content consists of group photos, inspirational quotes, and performance updates, yet lacks a unified visual identity. While audience interaction is low, they do respond to comments on Facebook. Their website has a bland design with a white background and minimal imagery. Though mobile-responsive and functional, it lacks engaging visuals and has broken links. There is no contact form, only social media links and an email for bookings.

International Playback Theatre Network (IPTN)
IPTN maintains a stronger social media presence on Instagram and Facebook, with posts every 1-2 months. Their content includes group photos, performance updates, event visuals, and interactive posts like surveys, with a consistent color scheme of blue and beige. They engage well with their audience, frequently responding to comments and promoting donation campaigns. Their website is visually appealing, well-organized, and easy to navigate. It effectively communicates the group’s mission and offers clear explanations of Playback Theatre, with pages dedicated to upcoming events and donation options.

I created a model in Figma for the visual appeal of the competitor analysis to present key insights in a clear and visually engaging manner. This approach was essential because it allowed me to highlight patterns and differences in competitors’ social media strategies, branding consistency, and online presence at a glance. By using a visual format, I was able to condense all the gathered data into an easily digestible reference, making it more effective for decision-making during the branding process.

The importance of this model lies in its ability to streamline the comparison of strengths and weaknesses across multiple competitors. It ensured that BLIK's branding strategy could be tailored to address gaps in the market while adopting practices that competitors had successfully implemented. Additionally, presenting the analysis visually made it easier for stakeholders to quickly grasp key findings and contribute to discussions on brand identity and content strategy.

To complement the survey data, a one-on-one interview was conducted with an event organizer, Eveline, who offered deeper insight into the needs and challenges of BLIK’s primary audience. Her feedback on website design, color schemes, and fonts played a pivotal role in refining the brand’s identity. The interview was recorded with Eveline's permission and with powerpoint slides, to be more clear with the questions i'm asking towards her. After analyzing the survey results, competitor analysis and interview, I created a business plan on the content strategy and visual identity to how Blik should represent themselves to their target audience.

The interview with Eveline was crucial for gaining insights into BLIK’s potential audience, brand perception, and event planning dynamics. By engaging directly with a professional who regularly organizes events and manages teams, the interview offered nuanced feedback that can guide BLIK in refining its offerings and improving its communication strategy. Eveline’s responses revealed valuable information about the importance of emotional engagement, creativity, and team-building in event planning, confirming that Playback Theatre could be a fitting option for the types of events she organizes.

Her candid feedback on BLIK’s website highlighted a critical area for improvement. While the design and colors were appealing, the homepage did not immediately convey that BLIK is a theatre group. This insight is significant, as first impressions are vital in attracting new clients. Additionally, Eveline’s preference for warm tones and an artistic font provided helpful guidance for aligning BLIK’s visual identity with its core values, ensuring consistency in its branding.

Overall, the interview underscored the need for clearer messaging, targeted outreach to event planners, and thoughtful design choices that emphasize BLIK’s unique approach to performance and engagement. Through this process, BLIK can better meet the expectations of its target audience and overcome potential barriers to booking performances.

The business plan for Blik Playback Theatre Group emphasizes the importance of establishing a strong online presence to address the group's current lack of visibility and accessibility for potential clients, such as HR professionals, event organizers, and private individuals. By creating a cohesive brand identity rooted in core values like reflection, playfulness, vulnerability, and connection, the plan aims to position Blik as a unique provider of emotionally impactful and team-building experiences.

Central to the strategy is the development of a professional, user-friendly website and the launch of social media platforms (Instagram, Facebook, and LinkedIn). These tools are designed to communicate Blik's mission, showcase its performances, and provide an accessible booking system. The branding strategy incorporates a green-focused color palette symbolizing growth and balance, paired with consistent visuals and messaging to foster recognition and engagement.

The plan underscores the importance of engaging content, including performance highlights, behind-the-scenes glimpses, and interactive posts, tailored to the preferences of Blik's target demographic. Insights from surveys and interviews guided the selection of posting schedules, platforms, and website features, ensuring alignment with audience expectations.

This approach not only addresses the visibility challenges but also differentiates Blik from competitors by focusing on brand consistency, high-quality visuals, and enhanced audience interaction. The business plan is a critical step in building awareness, driving bookings, and solidifying Blik's position as a leading Playback Theatre group.
